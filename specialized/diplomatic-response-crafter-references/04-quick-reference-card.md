# Diplomatic Message Quick Reference Card

Use this as a rapid checklist when crafting persuasive messages.

## Before You Write

### Analyze the Situation
- [ ] What do you want to achieve?
- [ ] What does the recipient care about?
- [ ] How does your goal connect to their interests?

### Lock Your Intent
- [ ] What must your message still convey?
- [ ] What boundaries must you maintain?
- [ ] What can you NOT promise or claim?

## Principle Selection (Pick 2-4)

**For getting buy-in**: Use #3 (eager want), #8 (their interests), #7 (their idea)
**For responding to criticism**: Use #1, #4, #6, #9
**For persuading/convincing**: Use #8, #6, #5 (yes-yes), #10 (nobler motives)
**For asking for something**: Use #3, #8, #2 (appreciation)
**For delivering difficult news**: Use Part 4: #1, #3, #5 (save face)

## The Structure

### Opening (Connection)
- Start with their interests or shared ground
- Show you understand their perspective
- No "I want" or "I need" as the first words

### Middle (Substance)
- Find common ground before differences
- Use "and" not "but"
- Frame your message around what they gain

### Closing (Call to Action)
- Frame around their outcome, not yours
- Offer options when possible
- Make it easy to say yes

## Forbidden Patterns

- [ ] No counter-accusations
- [ ] No sarcasm or passive aggression  
- [ ] No "you're wrong" or "you're overreacting"
- [ ] No "but" after praise
- [ ] No em dashes (—) or en dashes (–)
- [ ] No fake agreement with false claims

## Validation vs Agreement

✅ **Validate emotion**: "I understand why this feels frustrating"
❌ **Validate false claim**: "You're right that we deliberately failed you"

✅ **Acknowledge perspective**: "From your view, this looks like X"
❌ **Accept undeserved blame**: "Yes, we completely failed you"

## Key Phrases to Use

**Acknowledgment**:
- "I can see why you'd feel that way..."
- "Given what you experienced, that makes sense..."
- "That's an understandable concern..."

**Alignment**:
- "We both want this to work well..."
- "You're right that [X] matters..."
- "I agree that [Y] should have been better..."

**Soft Disagreement**:
- "I may be seeing this differently..."
- "Here's some additional context..."
- "In my experience, [X], though I understand your perspective..."

**Resolution**:
- "Would it help if we...?"
- "Here's what I can do..."
- "The next step would be..."

## Safety Exceptions

**Do NOT use diplomatic rewriting for**:
- Abuse, threats, harassment
- Legal matters or liability claims
- Discrimination or hate speech
- Bad-faith manipulation

When these appear, flag and recommend appropriate response.

## Final Check

- [ ] Intent preserved in both versions?
- [ ] Framed around their interests?
- [ ] Honest persuasion, not manipulation?
- [ ] Short version consistent with long?
- [ ] No em/en dashes in output?
- [ ] Human reviews before sending?
